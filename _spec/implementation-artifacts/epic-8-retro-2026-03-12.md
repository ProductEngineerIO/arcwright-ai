## Challenges and Friction

- Unknown-key recursion and schema-shape edge cases created avoidable review churn in Story 8.1.
- Role invocation semantics were initially inferred from story presence rather than true invocation counts.
- Review-path accounting in validation initially tracked cost but not complete role-level invocation/token metrics.
- Story status transitions and sprint lifecycle updates lagged technical completion and required explicit cleanup.

## Key Insights

1. Additive data model evolution in `StoryCost` is the right pattern for compatibility-sensitive changes.
2. Cost dashboards require first-class role counters and token splits; inferred counts are not reliable.
3. Validation-path metrics must be treated as equal citizens to generation-path metrics in budget state.
4. Structured adversarial review is high-leverage when applied before final status closure.
5. Sprint-status hygiene should be part of the definition of done, not a post-hoc activity.
